A small question

The sitch. My husband and I planned a trip to Vegas. Though winning would be amazing - knowing that the slot machine odds weren't in our favor - I reframed the question.

"How can I lose as little as possible, while still having fun gambling?"

The challenge became measuring my performance. I planned how much I was willing to lose and recorded where and when I played, the amount spent, and the amount made. The one rule was I could never spend my winnings.

Findings

The strategy worked. We walked away with $196.28 in profit.

Set limits. Having pre-set limits mean that you weren't tricked into trying to win back your money. Sometimes you win, sometimes you lose, but both were in moderation.

Location matters. I discovered that the majority of money was spent in our hotel. This map provides insight into where we should stay next time we visit.

  • Visualizing and sharing. R, design tools, social medias
